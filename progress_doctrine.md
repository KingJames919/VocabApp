# Progress Doctrine

## Status
**Approved direction through PASS 4.5. Visual and implementation changes remain future work.**

This document defines how we currently think about progress and momentum in the app.  
It should evolve only through scoped future passes after the current source-of-truth set is confirmed.

### PASS 4.5 direction note

- Path progress is orientation.
- Word progress and understanding progress are the meaningful learner signals.
- Final Recap is the main learner-facing progress surface.
- Progress should remain calm, provisional, and evidence-based.
- Internal `solid` / `fragile` values should display to learners as `Steady for now` / `Needs another look`.
- Use `transfer` internally when needed, but prefer `related word` / `new related word` in learner-facing copy.

---

## 1. Purpose

This doctrine exists to answer one question:

> **What does it mean for a learner to be making real progress in this product?**

It is used to guide:
- session copy and feedback
- flow and sequencing decisions
- future progress and momentum design
- what we choose to show (and not show) to the learner

---

## 2. Research Grounding

This product is grounded in two core ideas:

### A. Comprehension depends on knowledge, not generic strategies
From Hendrick:
- Students struggle to understand texts primarily because they **do not know enough of the words**, not because they lack strategies
- “Strategies” are often descriptions of failure, not causes of it
- Knowledge (vocabulary + domain understanding) is the **primary driver of comprehension**

**Implication for progress:**
Progress cannot be defined as “getting better at strategies.”  
It must reflect **growth in knowledge and meaning**.

---

### B. Vocabulary must be generative and cumulative
From Hendrick + morphology brief:
- Direct word teaching improves performance on **taught words**, but does not scale to overall comprehension
- Vocabulary knowledge becomes powerful when it is:
  - **generative** (supports new words)
  - **connected** (lives in families and schemas)
  - **cumulative** (builds over time)
- Morphology enables a **multiplicative model**: one base → many usable words

**Implication for progress:**
Progress must reflect:
- ability to **use and transfer** word knowledge
- growth in **word families**, not isolated words
- accumulation of **connected understanding**

---

### C. Knowledge builds slowly and compounds
From Hendrick:
- Knowledge-building is not a short intervention
- It is **gradual, cumulative, and compounding**

**Implication for progress:**
Progress should feel like **carry-forward growth**, not short-term completion.

---

## 3. Core Definition

> **Progress = growth in the knowledge that makes comprehension possible**

In this app, that specifically means:
- stronger command of anchor words
- stronger understanding of a morphology family
- improved ability to infer and use new words
- clearer understanding of the topic those words support

---

## 4. Three Layers of Progress (Working Model)

Progress is not one thing. It operates across three layers:

### 1. Path Progress (structural)
- Movement through Sessions 1–5
- Indicates **where the learner is**

**Role:** orientation only  
**Risk:** shallow if used alone

---

### 2. Word Progress (skill)
- Increasing ability to:
  - recognize
  - use
  - transfer
  anchor words and families

**Examples:**
- moving from recognition → correct use → flexible use
- applying a family clue to an untaught word

**Role:** core literacy growth

---

### 3. Understanding Progress (meaning)
- Increasing ability to explain and reason about the topic

**Examples (weather unit):**
- understanding pressure as a signal
- combining multiple data sources
- explaining how storms form

**Role:** primary goal of the product

---

## 5. What Progress Should Feel Like

Progress should feel like:

- “I understand more now”
- “I can use these words better”
- “I can explain something I couldn’t explain before”
- “This step built on the last one”

It should feel:
- cumulative
- calm
- meaningful
- connected to real understanding

---

## 6. What Progress Should NOT Be

Progress should **not** primarily be:

- task completion (“I finished Session 3”)
- correctness alone (“I got 3/3 right”)
- points, streaks, or rewards
- constant visual feedback loops
- abstract scores with no meaning

These may exist internally, but they are not the core signal.

---

## 7. Momentum Principle

Momentum comes from **evidence of growth**, not reward.

Strong signals:
- successful use of a word in context
- correct inference of an unfamiliar word
- clearer explanation of a concept
- improved precision or revision

Weak signals:
- generic praise (“Great job!”)
- completion badges
- visual progress bars without meaning

---

## 8. When to Show Progress (Timing Principle)

Progress should be **episodic, not constant**.

Best moments:
- end of a session
- after meaningful attempts
- recap
- occasional mid-session checkpoints

Avoid:
- continuous progress indicators
- interrupting thinking with feedback
- over-surfacing internal state

---

## 9. Product Bias (for V1)

During early development:

We intentionally bias toward:
- **under-showing progress**
- focusing on **content and learning clarity first**

Reason:
We do not yet fully understand what “growth” feels like in this system.

Progress should be **earned by the experience**, not added as a layer.

---

## 10. Immediate Implications (Current Passes)

### For copy (PASS 2–3):
- describe what the learner is learning, not just doing
- reinforce what is getting clearer or stronger
- avoid system/internal language

### For session flow (PASS 4):
- strengthen carry-forward between sessions
- make each step feel like it builds on the last
- tie tasks back to meaning, not just words

---

## 11. Open Questions (Intentionally Unresolved)

These will be decided later:

- How should progress be visualized?
- How much word-level status should learners see outside the Final Recap?
- How explicitly should knowledge growth be shown?
- What is the right balance between path vs word vs understanding signals?
- How frequently should progress be surfaced?

---

## 12. Future Work

### PASS 4.5 Direction

Approved direction:
- keep path progress as orientation, not the main signal
- use word progress and understanding progress as meaningful evidence
- keep the Final Recap as the main learner-facing progress surface
- defer visual progress system changes to future scoped work

---

## 13. Revision Trigger

This document should be revisited when:

- all sessions feel instructionally coherent
- learner flow feels cumulative
- we can clearly describe what “getting better” looks like in practice

---

## Bottom Line

> The product does not exist to show progress.  
> It exists to build knowledge.

Progress should only be surfaced when it reflects that truth.
